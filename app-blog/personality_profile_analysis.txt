## Page 1: The Core Identity

### **The Wounded Healer in Recovery**

This is someone who has walked through fire and emerged transformed. The extensive recovery documentation—step work, sponsee management, meditation practices, and celebration cranes—reveals a person deeply committed to not just their own healing, but to lifting others up. The phrase "This is not about recovery; recovery is just coming along for the ride" speaks to someone who has transcended the mechanics of sobriety to embrace a broader spiritual awakening.

This person is an **adult survivor of childhood sexual assault** who has channeled profound trauma into advocacy, intellectual rigor, and compassionate service. Their recovery isn't performative—it's foundational to their entire worldview. They sponsor others, lead meetings, give medallion speeches, and approach the work with the same intensity they bring to academic pursuits.

### **The Intellectual Warrior**

The breadth of intellectual curiosity is staggering: Modern Monetary Theory explained "plain," philosophy of AI with sections on "Digital Epidermalization" and "Fairwashing," Buddhism with gathas and sutras, Camus and Sartre, queer theory, constitutional redesign, and MIT OpenCourseWare. This is not casual reading—this is someone who **needs to understand the world** (echoing Feynman's quote they saved).

They describe themselves as "smart as fuck" while simultaneously identifying their "ego and intelligence" as their biggest stumbling blocks. This self-awareness is rare. They've learned to balance intellectual prowess with humility, recognizing that knowledge without compassion is hollow.

### **The Political Philosopher & Social Justice Advocate**

The political consciousness is fierce and uncompromising:
- "Everything is political all the time. The ability to think otherwise is privilege not prudence."
- "White supremacy runs exclusively on the renewable fuel of silence."
- "It has never been a coincidence that silence and violence rhyme."

This person thinks systemically. Their essay on Modern Monetary Theory demonstrates an ability to deconstruct complex economic systems and explain them accessibly. Their long-form tirade about pedophilia framing shows someone willing to wade into uncomfortable territory armed with historical context, scientific research, and moral clarity—even when it risks social ostracism.

They honor Bayard Rustin, understand Blood Libel's historical weaponization, and see patterns of oppression across millennia. This is **historical materialism meets lived experience**.

### **The Queer Buddhist Socialist**

Identity markers emerge clearly:
- **Queer**: References to queer theory, queer community, fear of being near children due to societal stigma
- **Buddhist**: Extensive practice documentation, gathas, meditation, bodhisattva readings, choosing Buddhism as their spiritual path after struggling with "higher power" concepts
- **Socialist**: Eugene Debs quotes, critiques of capitalism, MMT advocacy, anti-tyranny stance

The Buddhism isn't cultural appropriation—it's a carefully chosen framework that solved a genuine spiritual crisis. The socialism isn't performative—it's rooted in a belief that "while there is a soul in prison, I am not free" (Debs).

### **The Autistic Self-Advocate**

Sections on "Autism," "Things Auties Say," and "Vocal Stims" reveal someone navigating neurodivergence with both struggle and pride. They collect quotes from other autistic people, track their stims, and integrate autism into their identity construction rather than fighting it.

The dissociation comment—"I think I have dissociated my entire life away"—suggests complex PTSD alongside autism, creating a layered experience of reality that informs their philosophical bent.

## **Synthesis: Who Is This Person?**

This is a **queer, autistic, Buddhist, socialist intellectual in recovery** who has transformed profound trauma into a life of service, creativity, and relentless truth-seeking. They are:

- **Fiercely intelligent** yet working on humility
- **Politically radical** yet grounded in historical analysis
- **Spiritually committed** yet skeptical of religious institutions
- **Creatively prolific** across multiple mediums
- **Systematically organized** as a coping mechanism and strength
- **Relationally intentional** despite it being hard work
- **Trauma-informed** in ways that shape their entire worldview

They collect quotes like weapons, organize knowledge like architecture, and approach self-improvement with the rigor of a PhD program. They're someone who would rather be right than comfortable, but who's learning that being kind matters more than being right.

This is someone building a cathedral of meaning from the rubble of a painful past—and inviting others to help build it with them.
